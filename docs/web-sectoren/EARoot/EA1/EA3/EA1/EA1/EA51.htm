Motivation & Strategie
Motivation elements are used to model the motivations, or reasons, that guide the design or change of an Enterprise Architecture.<br/><br/><i>#lt;bron: </i><a href="https://pubs.opengroup.org/architecture/archimate32-doc/ch-Motivation-Elements.html"><font color="#0000ff"><i><u>hoofdstuk 6 - archimate 3.2 specificatie</u></i></font></a><i>#gt;</i><br/><i><br/></i>The strategy elements are typically used to model the strategic direction and choices of an enterprise, as far as the impact on its architecture is concerned. They can be used to express how the enterprise wants to create value for its stakeholders, the capabilities it needs, the resources needed to support these capabilities, as well as how it plans to configure and use these capabilities and resources to achieve its aims. Strategy elements are used to model the strategic direction and choices of the enterprise, whereas Business Layer elements are used to model the operational organization of an enterprise.<br/><br/><i>#lt;bron: </i><a href="https://pubs.opengroup.org/architecture/archimate32-doc/ch-Strategy-Layer.html"><font color="#0000ff"><i><u>hoofdstuk 7 - archimate 3.2 specificatie</u></i></font></a><i>#gt;</i><br/><br/>
